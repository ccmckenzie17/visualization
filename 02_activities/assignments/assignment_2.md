      Visualization 1: "Less Education For Your Buck".
      This visualization demonstrates how college tuition has outpaced inflation in the US by 6x since 1980. While inflation rose by 228% over that timespan, tutition increased by +1184%. 
      Nice to look at? 
      Yes! This chart is simple and visually appealling with a very clear message. The two lines are clearly labelled and seperated by colour. The two axis aren't explicitly titles but the units are clear (percentage increase on y and year on x). Faint lines are used in the background to help line the data up to the y axis but don't distract from the other features.
      Accurate?
      Yes! The data is clearly labelled as being from the US Bureau of Labor Statistics and is verifiable through there publicly avaialbe databases. The data is presented in an accurate way that does not mislead the viewer. 
      What's the message?
      The message is that tuition costs have increased much more than would be expected by just inflation since 1980 in the US. This message is clear and while it does tell a story that suggests to the viewer that college tuition is too expensive, it does not impose a specific interpretation or show obvious bias.
      Overall good or bad?
      Good! This visualization is clear, accurate, and informative.

      Visualization 2: "How fast did it sell?"
      This visualization compares how much different game consoles have sold.
      Nice to look at?
      Yes. This visualization does have some initial visual appeal with nice graphics, the use of logos, and a message that at first does seem clear. However, a closer look shows some poor design choices. First, This data should be presented in a bar chart with a clearly defined y axis because it is not clear what the scale is. Second, units are not clearly defined and the numbers could be interpreted at dollars (or maybe thousands of dollars or something similar) or units of consoles.
      Accurate?
      Yes? While it does appear that the sales numbers are accurate (Correct number of nintendo switch sales in 4 days (worldwide) from a nintendo press release https://www.cnn.com/2025/06/11/tech/nintendo-switch-2-sales-record), the accuracy is questionable in other ways. First, the decision to normalize sales to per day but on different sales (from 4 days to 4.5 yrs) completely obsures the point as all consoles will sell much more soon after their initial release so these daily sales numbers will very likely change over time. While the Switch 2 has sold about twice as much as the Switch 1 on a time-aligned scale (https://www.gamespot.com/articles/switch-2-reached-new-sales-heights-in-the-us-in-2025/1100-6537566/), that is very different than the almost 10x difference presented in this visualization. Second, no source is provided for the data and it seems like they compiled disperate press release numbers instead of a more detailed or verifiable data soure. Finally, because the source is not clear it is not obvious at first if these are US domestic sales or worldwide. 
      What's the message?
      That the Switch 2 is selling very well compared to these 4 other consoles.
      Overall good or bad?
      Bad. This visualization is misleading and prioritizes presenting a biased message over communication data clearly.
